# Community Guidelines

Grid exists to give gay and bi men a free, judgment-free space to connect — for dating, hookups, friendship, and community. These guidelines keep it that way.

## Be Real
Use real, recent photos of yourself. Fake profiles, catfishing, and impersonation get you banned. Verified badges exist to make the grid more trustworthy — use them.

## Consent Is Non-Negotiable
Only post photos or videos of yourself, or of others who have clearly and knowingly agreed to appear on Grid. Sharing someone's private content without their consent — including screenshots from chats — is a permanent ban and may be reported to the platform hosting the harmed party's legal recourse.

## No Minors, Ever
Grid is 18+ only. Any account, content, or conversation suggesting involvement of anyone under 18 is removed immediately, the account is permanently banned, and we report it to the relevant authority. This is the one rule with zero tolerance and no appeals process.

## Respect the Grid
- No harassment, hate speech, or threats — including based on race, body type, HIV status, or any other personal attribute.
- No unsolicited explicit content in first messages — send a tap or a message first.
- Health-status and PrEP fields are there so people can make informed choices — don't use them to harass or shame anyone.
- Block and report tools exist for a reason. Use them; we take every report seriously.

## Keep It Legal
No solicitation of commercial sexual services where illegal in your location, no drug sales, no content illegal in the UK or Germany. Explicit content is welcome between consenting verified adults, within the limits set out in our Terms of Service.

## Reviews Are for Feedback, Not Payback
If you've confirmed meeting someone, you can leave them a review. Keep it honest and relevant to your actual interaction — no real names, addresses, workplaces, or other identifying details beyond what's already on their profile, and never use a review to out, threaten, or retaliate against someone. Reviews sit invisible until the other person approves them, and they choose whether an approved review shows publicly or stays private — that's their call, not yours.

## Meeting Up Safely
- Video chat before meeting in person where possible.
- Tell a friend where you're going.
- Meet in a public place first if you're unsure.
- Trust your instincts — you can always leave.

## Enforcement

Violations may result in content removal, temporary suspension, or permanent ban, depending on severity. Reports involving minors or illegal content are escalated immediately and are not subject to appeal.

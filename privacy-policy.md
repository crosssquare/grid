This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, share, and protect your personal data when you use Grid, in compliance with the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018, and applicable German data protection law (BDSG).

## 1. What We Collect

| Category | Examples | Why |
|---|---|---|

## 2. Special Category Data

Health status and similar fields are **entirely optional** and entered by you. Under Article 9 GDPR, we process this only on the basis of your **explicit consent**, captured separately from our general terms. You can remove this information from your profile at any time, and doing so deletes it from our systems (subject to backup retention described in §7). We never use this data for advertising — we do not run advertising — and we exclude it from any bulk data export we might provide to a third party (it is included, of course, in your personal GDPR data export to *you*).

## 3. Legal Bases for Processing

- **Contract** (Art. 6(1)(b)): account creation, core app functionality
- **Consent** (Art. 6(1)(a) / Art. 9(2)(a)): location sharing, cookies, health-status fields, marketing communications
- **Legitimate interest** (Art. 6(1)(f)): fraud prevention, anti-spam, platform security
- **Legal obligation** (Art. 6(1)(c)): age verification, responding to lawful requests from authorities, reporting suspected illegal content (including CSAM) to the relevant national authority

## 4. Age Verification

Because Grid includes explicit content, we are legally required (under the UK Online Safety Act 2023 and the German Interstate Treaty on the Protection of Minors in the Media — JMStV) to apply robust age assurance. We use a third-party verification provider to confirm you are 18+. **We do not receive or store your ID document or biometric scan** — our provider returns only a pass/fail result and a reference token, which is what we store.

## 5. Who We Share Data With

- **Sub-processors:** cloud hosting, CDN, the age-verification vendor, our payment processor, transactional email provider — each bound by a Data Processing Agreement.
- **Other users:** only what you choose to make public on your profile, and only messages you choose to send.
- **Our moderation team:** if you or someone you've messaged files a report, our moderation staff may review the relevant message content and media strictly to investigate that report. This access is logged and limited to what's necessary to resolve the report.
- **Law enforcement / regulators:** when legally required, including mandatory reporting of suspected child sexual abuse material, which we detect via automated hash-matching on every upload.
- **We do not sell your data, and we do not share it with advertisers, because we do not run advertising.**

## 6. International Transfers

Where data is processed outside the UK/EEA (e.g. by a cloud sub-processor), we rely on the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses (or the UK's International Data Transfer Addendum) and require equivalent safeguards.

## 7. Retention

- Active account data: retained while your account is active.
- Deleted accounts: enter a 30-day soft-delete window (recoverable), after which data is permanently purged, except where we must retain limited records to comply with legal obligations (e.g. fraud/report records tied to a moderation action) for up to [X] months.
- Messages: retained until deleted by you or your account, or per our standard retention window of [X] months for inactive conversations.

## 8. Your Rights

Under GDPR/UK GDPR, you have the right to: access your data, rectify inaccurate data, erase your data ("right to be forgotten"), restrict processing, data portability, object to processing, and withdraw consent at any time. You can exercise all of these yourself, instantly, from **Profile → Data & Privacy**, or contact our Data Protection Officer at [DPO EMAIL]. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority (in the UK: the ICO; in Germany: your state's Landesbeauftragte für Datenschutz, or the BfDI).

## 9. Security

We use encryption in transit (TLS) and at rest, column-level encryption for special-category fields, role-based access control for staff, and mandatory automated scanning of uploaded media against known illegal-content hash databases prior to publication.

## 10. Children

Grid is strictly for users aged 18 and over. We do not knowingly collect data from anyone under 18, and we operate the age-assurance system described in §4 to prevent it.
